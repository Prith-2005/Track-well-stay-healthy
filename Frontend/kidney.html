How Obesity Affects the Kidneys

Source : Internet

1. Increases the Risk of Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

Higher Filtration Load: Excess body fat increases the workload on the kidneys, causing them to filter more blood than normal (a condition called hyperfiltration). Over time, this can damage the kidney's filtering units (nephrons).

Glomerular damage: The increased pressure in glomeruli (tiny filters in kidneys) can lead to glomerulosclerosisâ€”scarring of these filters, reducing kidney function.

2. Promotes Conditions That Harm the Kidneys

Obesity is a major risk factor for diseases that directly damage the kidneys, including:

  • Type 2 Diabetes: High blood sugar can damage kidney blood vessels and lead to diabetic nephropathy.
  • Hypertension (High Blood Pressure): Extra weight increases blood pressure, which can damage kidney vessels and accelerate kidney disease.
  • Dyslipidemia: Abnormal levels of fats in the blood can contribute to atherosclerosis (hardening of arteries), which reduces kidney perfusion.

3. Leads to Kidney Stones

Obesity changes the acid-base balance and increases uric acid and calcium excretion, both of which increase the risk of kidney stones.

4. Associated with Obesity-Related Glomerulopathy (ORG)

This is a kidney condition specifically linked to obesity, where the kidneys develop enlarged glomeruli and show signs of damage, often leading to proteinuria (protein in the urine) and gradual loss of function.

5. Complicates Kidney Transplant and Dialysis Outcomes

Obesity can make it harder to receive or recover from a kidney transplant and increases the risk of complications in dialysis patients.

Can Weight Loss Help?

Yes. Losing weight through lifestyle changes, medications, or surgery can:

  • Reduce hyperfiltration
  • Lower blood pressure and blood sugar
  • Improve proteinuria
  • Slow down progression of CKD
