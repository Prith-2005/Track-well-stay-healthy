How Many Calories Does Your Body Burn Naturally Each Day?

Source : Internet

Introduction

Your body burns calories every day just to keep you alive—this is known as your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). BMR is the number of calories your body uses to maintain essential functions like breathing, circulation, cell production, and temperature regulation while at rest.

What Is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

BMR accounts for about 60–75% of the total calories you burn daily. It varies depending on factors such as:

  • Age: Metabolism slows down with age.
  • Gender: Males typically have higher BMR due to more muscle mass.
  • Body Size and Composition: More muscle = higher calorie burn.
  • Genetics: Your inherited traits can influence metabolism.

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

The actual number of calories you burn each day is called your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). It includes:

  • BMR – calories burned at rest.
  • Physical Activity: Exercise and movement throughout the day.
  • Thermic Effect of Food (TEF): Energy used to digest, absorb, and process food (about 10% of calorie intake).

Estimating Your BMR

You can estimate your BMR using formulas like the Harris-Benedict Equation:

  • For Men: BMR = 88.36 + (13.4 × weight in kg) + (4.8 × height in cm) – (5.7 × age in years)
  • For Women: BMR = 447.6 + (9.2 × weight in kg) + (3.1 × height in cm) – (4.3 × age in years)

How Many Calories Does the Average Person Burn?

The average daily calorie burn (TDEE) for adults is:

  • Women: 1,800 to 2,200 calories per day
  • Men: 2,200 to 2,800 calories per day

This varies widely based on activity level. Sedentary individuals burn less, while very active people can burn significantly more.

Conclusion

Understanding how many calories your body naturally burns helps with weight management, fitness goals, and overall health. Calculating your BMR and TDEE is a great first step toward creating a balanced nutrition and exercise plan tailored to your body’s needs.
